What This Looks Like

Three moments where
Career Hub changes the outcome.

Real situations. What happens when your team has visibility versus when they don't.

Situation: A strong employee is quietly checking out. She hasn't said anything. Her work is still solid. But she's been in the same role for 26 months, her manager hasn't brought up growth in two quarters, and she's started clicking on job alerts again.

Without Career Hub: you find out at the resignation conversation.
With Career Hub: her manager sees her path engagement drop, the system flags it, and the 1:1 that week opens with "here's what VP looks like for someone in your position."
The outcome
She doesn't leave. You don't spend 6 months and $40K replacing someone you could have kept with one conversation backed by data.
Situation: You have a req open. You're about to post it externally. It's a senior operations role. You assume there's no one internal — the team is small and you hired for specific functions, not breadth.

Career Hub's Internal Mobility Engine surfaces two employees who've been actively exploring this role type. One has already completed 80% of the required skills pathway on her own time.
The outcome
You skip the external search. She's hired in three weeks at a fraction of the cost. The role she vacates is a more junior backfill — cheaper and easier to fill externally.
Situation: A skill is becoming critical in your industry. Your team doesn't have it. Career Hub's skills intelligence is pulling from real-time LinkedIn data. It flags that AI proficiency just became a top-3 trending skill for operations roles in financial services — and only 9% of your operations team has it.

Your competitors don't know this yet. They'll find out when they try to hire for it next year and can't.
The outcome
You roll out a targeted AI learning path to your ops team this quarter. Six months from now, your workforce has the skill your competitors are scrambling to hire for. That's not reacting to the market — that's staying ahead of it.
