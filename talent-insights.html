Wrong offer. Lost candidate. Three months of recruiting time. Back to zero. These aren't edge cases — they're what happens when your data is older than your last salary review.

You're hiring a senior financial analyst in Denver. You offer $165K based on last year's Mercer survey. The candidate declines. What This Looks Like

Intelligence that changes
how you make decisions.

Three scenarios. Real situations. What happens when your team has the data versus when they don't.

Scenario: You're losing advisors and don't know why. Your attrition is up. Exit interviews say "growth opportunities" — but that's what everyone says. You need to know if this is a you problem or a market problem.

Talent Insights shows your top competitor's average advisor tenure just dropped from 4.2 years to 2.8 years. Their comp is running 8% below market. Their people are restless.
The intelligence move
You target that firm's advisors with precision outreach — not a job posting. You know who's ready to move before they've told anyone. That's not recruiting. That's intelligence.
Scenario: Leadership wants to open a new office. They've picked Dallas. The decision is based on where the VP went to college and where the company already has clients. No talent data has been reviewed.

You pull a Talent Insights report. The skill cluster you need is denser in Austin. Comp runs 12% lower. Competition for the same profiles is meaningfully less intense.
The intelligence move
You bring data to a gut-feel decision — and change the outcome. TA stops being order-takers and becomes the team that saves the company $400K on a bad location call.
Scenario: You've got a req open for 90 days. No qualified applicants. The JD asks for 8 years of experience in a technology that's only existed for 5. Nobody flagged it. The hiring manager wrote it based on their last job at a Fortune 50 company.

Talent Insights shows the actual experience distribution for this role. The realistic pool requires 3–4 years. The JD has been screening out 80% of qualified candidates before they ever applied.
The intelligence move
You fix the JD with data — not a conversation. The req fills in three weeks. The hiring manager now asks TA to validate every JD before it posts.
